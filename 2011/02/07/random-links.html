[The case against queues...](http://teddziuba.com/2011/02/the-case-against-queues.html) > Depending on your queue implementation, when you pop a message off, it's gone. The consumer acknowledges receipt of it, and the queue forgets about it. So, if your data processor fails, you've got data loss. Collecting messages [with] syslog, your processor program is just processing a text file, and can process it again if something goes wrong. Throw in some split and xargs, and you've got parallel processing. Event messages aren't text? You fucked up. Go buy a subscription to the Microsoft Developer Network.
